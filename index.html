Scenario: Business Trip Planning

You are planning a business trip to Chicago next month for a conference. Your colleague Maria has been to Chicago several times and knows the city well.

Task: Write an email to Maria asking for recommendations. In your email, you should:

  • Explain the purpose of your trip
  • Ask for restaurant recommendations

Module 3: Academic Discussion

Read the discussion topic and student responses, then contribute your own response.

Time Remaining: 10:00
Academic Discussion 10 minutes

Professor's Question

This week, we are discussing the rise of digital nomadism - the practice of working remotely while traveling. Some argue that this lifestyle represents the future of work, offering freedom and flexibility. Others are concerned about its impact on productivity, work-life balance, and local communities.

Do you think digital nomadism is a positive development? Why or why not?

Student Responses

David:

I believe digital nomadism is great for personal growth. Traveling exposes you to new cultures and perspectives, which can actually make you more creative at work. However, I do think it requires strong self-discipline to stay productive without a traditional office environment.

Sophia:

I'm more skeptical about digital nomadism. While it sounds appealing, I worry about the impact on local communities in popular destinations. Rising rents and gentrification can displace locals when areas become popular with remote workers. Also, constantly moving makes it hard to build deep relationships or feel part of a community.

Express your opinion on digital nomadism. You can agree or disagree with the students above, or present a different perspective. Support your position with reasons and examples.

Word count: 0 | Suggested: 100+ words
