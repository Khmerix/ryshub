Atmospheric Layers

Earth's atmosphere is a blanket of gases held by gravity, composed of ~78% Nitrogen, 21% Oxygen, and 1% other gases.

🌤️ Troposphere — 0 to 12 km

Where all weather happens. Temperature decreases with altitude. Contains ~75% of atmospheric mass.

✈️ Stratosphere — 12 to 50 km

Contains the Ozone Layer which absorbs harmful UV radiation. Jets fly here for smooth travel.

☄️ Mesosphere — 50 to 80 km

Meteors burn up here creating "shooting stars." Coldest layer with temperatures dropping to -90°C.

🛰️ Thermosphere — 80 to 700 km

ISS and many satellites orbit here. Aurora Borealis occurs in this layer. Temperatures reach 2,000°C+ but feels cold due to low particle density.

🌌 Exosphere — 700 to 10,000 km

Outermost layer fading into space. Very few atoms, satellites like GPS orbit in the lower exosphere.

🌑 Synchronous Rotation

The Moon rotates exactly once per orbit, which is why we only ever see one face from Earth.

🌊 Tidal Forces

The Moon's gravity creates ocean tides, slowing Earth's rotation and gradually pushing the Moon 3.8 cm farther away each year.

🌙 Phases

Moon phases result from the angle between Sun, Moon, and Earth — not Earth's shadow (that's a lunar eclipse).

🚀 Exploration

12 humans have walked on the Moon (Apollo 11-17, 1969-1972). NASA's Artemis program plans to return by 2026.
