BLACKWOOD MANOR

A Rain-Slicked Night

The Case

📷 Place manor.png in /images

Lord Blackwood lies dead in his locked study. The grandfather clock struck midnight an hour ago, and the storm has washed out all roads. No one leaves until dawn.

You are the detective. The household consists of:

Lady Eleanor Blackwood
The victim's wife. Young, elegant, and reportedly unhappy in her marriage.
Dr. Marcus Vance
Family physician. Last person to see Lord Blackwood alive.
Thomas the Butler
Loyal servant of twenty years. Knows every lock and passage in the manor.

Investigation

Interrogation

The clock ticks. You must choose who to press for answers.

The Final Accusation

You gather the household in the drawing room. The storm howls outside. You have one chance to name the killer.

✓ ENDING 1: JUSTICE SERVED

📷 Use thomas.png or justice_ending.png

You present the muddy boot prints and the poisoned tea. Thomas's composure shatters.

"He was going to dismiss me after my wife's funeral! I only meant to lock him in. The tea was for his nerves. I didn't know about the cyanide!"

The truth unravels: Thomas tampered with the study lock, but Dr. Vance had poisoned the tea to cover up medical malpractice. Both are taken into custody.

The storm breaks at dawn. You solved it cleanly. Case closed.

⚠ ENDING 2: HALF-TRUTH

You accuse Thomas, but your evidence is incomplete. He admits to tampering with the lock but denies murder.

Without the poison clue, the death is ruled a heart attack. Thomas is dismissed without charge.

You uncovered part of the truth, but the full picture remains in the shadows.

✗ ENDING 3: THE SHADOW REMAINS

Your accusation falters. The constable arrives, shrugs, and files it as natural causes.

As you pack your coat, you notice a familiar boot print drying on the hallway rug... and a half-empty teacup waiting to be washed.

Some mysteries outlive their detectives.
